Suriname....

The country where different cultures come together and make mix babies. Yes, we like to joke around as well.

Suriname is the tiniest sovereign state maped on the northeast side of South-America. Our mother tongue is Dutch (not Deutsch(german, though) and that is the official language of the country as well. Suprised?... From the 16th century until the half of the 19th century, Suriname was a colony of the Netherlands. After that she became a country under the Dutch Kingdom Asociation.

On the 25th of November 1975, Suriname gained its independence and became officially known as the Republic of Suriname.

(image: Independence celebration)

This country has a rich culture diversity. Basically, every race you can find in Suriname. From African to Chinese to Indonesian to Indian and even Native Americans, etc. You can call her the Surinamese version of the Tomorrowland event. Every race in one small bucket living in harmony. A blessed country if you would ask me. It is very common that other races learn how to speak each others language. The most common that is spoken other than Dutch, is Sranan' Tongo (surinamese tongue) which was created during slavery by the descendants of Africa.
